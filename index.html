Ray Allen NBA Career Timeline

Overview: Ray Allen was born July 20th, 1975 and is listed as 6ft 5in. He went to Hillcrest high school in Dalzell, South Carolina then went to UConn college from 1993-1996. He also played basketball in college at the shooting guard position, he is known as one of the greatest shooter ever.

Draft Day: In 1996 Ray Allen was drafted to the NBA by the Timberwolves with the 5th overall pick in the first round but, was immediately traded to the Bucks wearing number 34.

Milwaukee Bucks: Allen played for the Bucks from 1996-2003, the 7 seasons he played with them overall he averaged 19.6 points per game playing 494 games but unfortunately he did not win his first championship there.

Seattle SuperSonics: After spending some time in Milwaukee, Allen was traded to the SuperSonics where he played 5 seaons playing 296 games. He averaged 24.6 points per game, the highest in his career but still no championship yet. I beieve while he was with the SuperSonics he had his breakout year.

Boston Celtics: Once again Allen was traded this time to the Celtics where he played 358 games averaging 16.7 points per game over the course of another 5 seasons (2007-2012). The Celtics had good basketball players around Ray such as Rajon Rondo, Kevin Garnett, Paul Perice, Shaquille O'Neal etc. On June 17th, 2008 in a series-ending Game 6 of the NBA Finals Ray tied an NBA Finals record with seven three-pointers in the Celtics' 131-92 victory over the Los Angeles Lakers. The victory secured Ray his first NBA championship. While he was with the Celtics he began to wear number 20 as well.

Miami Heat: Ray then joined the Heat in free agency bbecause of the Celtics' rumored trade talks, major pay cuts, and wasn't getting along with teammates. Allen joined the Heat and played 152 games in 2 seasons, averaging 10.3 points per game which is the lowest in his career as he began to age. Allen had many superstar players around his again such as Lebron James, Dwayne Wade, Chris Bosh etc.
